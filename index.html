About Us

About Repowering Partners

A dedicated renewable energy infrastructure company focused on acquiring, repowering, and operating solar assets for long-term value creation.

Company Overview

Infrastructure Investors Focused on Solar Energy

Repowering Partners was founded to address a growing opportunity in the renewable energy sector: the acquisition and revitalization of existing solar assets. As the solar industry matures, thousands of solar farms across North America are reaching an age where they benefit from new ownership and capital investment.

Our team brings together decades of experience in energy infrastructure, project finance, and renewable energy operations.

Our Mission

Extending the Life of Solar Infrastructure

Our mission is to maximize the productive life and financial returns of existing solar energy infrastructure. By acquiring assets that are underperforming, aging, or simply ready for new ownership, we breathe new life into these critical energy assets.

We believe that the most sustainable approach to clean energy is not just building new — it's also maintaining, upgrading, and optimizing what already exists.

How It Works

Our Acquisition Process

A streamlined, transparent process designed to move quickly while ensuring thorough evaluation of every asset.

Step by Step

Four Steps to Closing

We've refined our acquisition process to be as efficient and transparent as possible for asset owners.

1

Submit Asset Information

Begin by sharing basic information about your solar farm — location, capacity, year built, and current performance data. You can use our online form or contact our team directly. All information is treated as strictly confidential.

2

Preliminary Evaluation & Indicative Offer

Our team reviews your asset's characteristics and provides an initial assessment. If the asset fits our investment criteria, we issue a non-binding indicative offer, typically within 5–10 business days.

3

Due Diligence

Upon mutual agreement to proceed, we conduct comprehensive technical, financial, and legal due diligence. This includes equipment inspections, production data analysis, contract review, and environmental assessments.

4

Transaction Closing

Following successful due diligence, we finalize transaction documents and execute the closing. Ownership transfer and payment occur simultaneously.
